Feature Phone Revolution

This chapter covers the era when mobile phones became mainstream—compact, colorful, and often indestructible. Simplicity met innovation before the rise of smartphones.

Legendary Devices

  • Nokia 3310 (2000)
  • Sony Ericsson K750i
  • Motorola RAZR V3
  • Samsung SGH-D500
  • Siemens A55
  • LG Chocolate series

Popular Features

  • Color screens and polyphonic ringtones
  • T9 predictive text input
  • Basic cameras (VGA and 1.3 MP)
  • FM radios and MP3 playback
  • Games like Snake, Bounce, and Sudoku
  • Removable batteries and expandable storage

Standout Moments

  • 1999 – Nokia 3210 sells over 160 million units
  • 2004 – Motorola RAZR becomes a fashion icon
  • 2006 – Sony Ericsson Walkman phones blend music and mobility
  • WAP internet lets users check emails and limited web
