Early Mobile Era

The rise of mobile communication began here—with enormous handsets, limited battery life, and sky-high calling costs, but also revolutionary freedom.

Key Devices

  • Motorola DynaTAC 8000X (1983)
  • Nokia Cityman 900 (1987)
  • NEC 9A (1987)
  • Ericsson Hotline 450
  • Mobira Senator (car phone)

Technologies & Features

  • Analog 1G Networks (AMPS, NMT, TACS)
  • Large removable batteries
  • Physical antennas
  • Very limited contact storage
  • No text messaging or data connectivity

Notable Milestones

  • 1973 – First mobile call made by Martin Cooper on a prototype DynaTAC
  • 1983 – DynaTAC 8000X becomes first commercially available mobile phone
  • 1985 – UK launches mobile services with Cellnet and Vodafone
  • 1987 – First GSM standard proposal drafted
